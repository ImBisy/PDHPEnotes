🤝 1. Relationships

🌟 1.1 Key Qualities

  • Trust: 🤲 Developed over time through consistent honesty, reliability, and keeping promises. Trust is essential for feeling safe and secure in a relationship, and it allows people to be vulnerable without fear of betrayal.
  • Respect: 🙇‍♂️ Involves acknowledging and valuing the differences, boundaries, and beliefs of others. Respect means accepting others' opinions without judgment, listening carefully, and treating everyone as equals regardless of disagreements.
  • Empathy: 💞 The ability to put oneself in another person's shoes and genuinely understand their feelings and perspectives. Empathy leads to support, emotional connection, and helps to resolve conflicts constructively.
  • Honesty: 🗣️ Being truthful and open in all communications, even when it is difficult. Honesty builds transparency in the relationship and prevents misunderstandings or mistrust.
  • Support: 🫂 Providing emotional, informational, or practical assistance. This includes listening actively, giving advice when appropriate, and being there during both good and challenging times.

👥 1.2 Types

  • Family: 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 The first social unit we interact with, which shapes values, beliefs, and communication styles. Family relationships involve care, protection, and guidance, and can include parents, siblings, extended relatives, and caregivers.
  • Friendships: 👫 Voluntary relationships based on shared interests, experiences, trust, and mutual support. Friends provide companionship, emotional security, and opportunities for social development.
  • Romantic: 💑 Relationships that involve emotional and physical intimacy. These require clear boundaries, mutual consent, respect, and communication to ensure both people feel valued and safe.
  • Professional: 💼 Relationships formed in work or educational settings. Professional relationships are grounded in respect, clear boundaries, effective communication, and collaboration to achieve shared goals.

🗣️ 2. Power & Communication

⚖️ 2.1 Power Dynamics

  • Balanced: 🤲 Both individuals have equal say in decisions, share control, and respect each other’s needs and perspectives. Balanced power fosters cooperation and mutual respect.
  • Imbalanced: ⛔ One person holds more control, leading to dominance, manipulation, or coercion. Signs include one-sided decision-making, isolation, threats, or emotional/physical abuse. Imbalanced power can harm relationships and personal wellbeing.

💬 2.2 Communication Skills

  • Active Listening: 👂 Fully concentrating, understanding, and responding thoughtfully to what someone says. Techniques include nodding, paraphrasing for clarification, and avoiding interruption.
  • Assertive: 💪 Expressing thoughts, feelings, and needs honestly and respectfully using “I” statements (e.g., “I feel…”). Assertive communicators stand up for themselves without violating others’ rights.
  • Non-Verbal: 🧏 Communication through body language, facial expressions, eye contact, and tone of voice. Non-verbal cues can reinforce or contradict verbal messages and are crucial for understanding intent.
  • Feedback: 🔄 Providing or seeking constructive responses to clarify understanding, correct errors, or praise efforts. Good feedback is specific, respectful, and focused on behaviors rather than personal traits.

🤔 2.3 Conflict Resolution

  1. 🕵️‍♂️ Identify the issue: Clearly define what the conflict is about and ensure all parties understand the problem.
  2. 🧘 Stay calm—use breaks if needed: Manage emotions to prevent escalation. If things get heated, take time to cool off before continuing.
  3. 🗣️ Express via “I” statements: Communicate feelings and needs without blaming the other person (e.g., “I feel upset when…”).
  4. 💡 Brainstorm solutions: Collaboratively generate possible ways to address the issue, considering everyone’s needs.
  5. ✍️ Agree on a plan: Select the most appropriate solution and ensure all parties are willing to commit.
  6. 🔄 Follow up and adjust: Revisit the agreement after some time to check if it’s working and make changes if necessary.

⚠️ 3. Risk-Taking

🧩 3.1 Definition

Risk-taking is the conscious or unconscious decision to expose oneself to the possibility of harm or loss. Risks can be physical, emotional, social, or legal, and are measured by the likelihood of a negative outcome and the potential severity of consequences. Not all risks are negative—some can lead to growth, learning, or positive change. 🎯

🤷‍♂️ 3.2 Reasons

  • Thrill-seeking: 🎢 Some individuals are motivated by excitement, adrenaline, or curiosity and take risks for enjoyment or novelty.
  • Peer pressure: 👬 Desire to fit in or gain approval from friends can lead to risky behaviors, especially among adolescents.
  • Escape/Coping: 🏃‍♂️ People may engage in risk-taking as a way to distract from stress, anxiety, or difficult emotions.
  • Self-exploration: 🔍 Taking risks can be part of personal growth, testing limits, or learning new skills.

Consequences: ⚖️ Risk-taking can have positive or negative outcomes. Positive risks (e.g., trying a new sport, public speaking) foster growth, while negative risks (e.g., substance abuse) can have serious repercussions.

💊 4. Drugs

📖 4.1 Definitions

Drug: 🧠 Any substance that, when taken into the body, alters its physical or psychological functioning. This includes legal drugs (e.g., caffeine, alcohol, prescription medications) and illegal drugs (e.g., cocaine, heroin).

Drug Misuse: ⚠️ Using a drug in a way that is not intended or prescribed, which can lead to harm or health risks.

Drug Abuse: 🚫 The repeated, intentional use of drugs in a way that is harmful to oneself or others, often leading to addiction, health problems, or legal issues.

Dependence: 🔄 A state in which the body or mind relies on a drug to function normally, often resulting in tolerance and withdrawal symptoms when not using the drug.

📚 4.2 Classifications & Uses

Type Description Examples Uses
Depressant 💤 Depressants slow down the central nervous system (CNS), reducing arousal and stimulation. They can cause drowsiness, relaxation, and decreased inhibition, but also impair coordination and judgment. Alcohol, Cannabis (in some forms), Heroin, Benzodiazepines Used medically for sleep induction, anxiety relief, and muscle relaxation. Recreational use can lead to misuse and addiction.
Hallucinogen 🌈 Hallucinogens alter perception, mood, and thought processes. They can cause hallucinations (seeing or hearing things that are not there) and distort reality. LSD, Magic Mushrooms, Ecstasy (MDMA, also has stimulant effects) Some being researched for use in treating PTSD, depression, and anxiety. Recreational use is illegal and can have unpredictable effects.
Stimulant ⚡ Stimulants speed up the CNS, increasing alertness, energy, and confidence. They can also raise heart rate and blood pressure, and reduce appetite. Caffeine, Nicotine, Cocaine, Amphetamines Used for ADHD treatment, to stay awake, or recreationally. Chronic use can cause dependence and serious health problems.

🧪 4.3 Effects Comparison

Drug Short-Term Long-Term
Alcohol 🍺 Slurred speech, impaired coordination, lowered inhibitions, mood changes. Risk of accidents and poisoning with excessive use. Chronic liver and brain damage, increased cancer risk, cardiovascular issues, addiction (alcoholism), social and legal problems.
Cannabis 🌿 Red eyes, slowed reaction time, altered perception, impaired short-term memory. Some people may feel anxious or paranoid. Memory/cognitive issues, decreased motivation, risk of mental health disorders (e.g., psychosis), respiratory problems if smoked, dependence.
Nicotine 🚬 Increased heart rate, dizziness, mild euphoria. Highly addictive—users can develop dependence quickly. Heart disease, lung cancer, respiratory illness, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), stroke, persistent addiction.
Ecstasy 😃 Euphoria, increased sociability, enhanced sensory perception, dehydration and overheating, jaw clenching. Depression, memory problems, sleep disturbances, potential long-term brain changes, risk of organ damage.
Cocaine ❄️ Euphoria, high energy, alertness, increased confidence, but also anxiety and paranoia. High risk of overdose. Heart problems (arrhythmias, attacks), severe addiction, mood disorders, nasal damage (if snorted), financial/legal issues.
Ice (Methamphetamine) 🧊 Sudden bursts of energy, aggression, rapid breathing, decreased appetite, hallucinations. Psychosis, severe brain damage, dental issues (“meth mouth”), violent behavior, high risk of dependence.

🛡️ 4.4 Harm Minimisation Strategies

  • Education: 📚 Accurate information helps people make informed decisions and understand risks.
  • Safer Use: 🩹 If using, avoid mixing substances, know the dose, and never use alone.
  • Support Services: ☎️ Seek help from counselors, hotlines, or support groups if struggling with substance use.
  • Legal Consequences: ⚖️ Be aware of the laws regarding possession, supply, and use of drugs.
  • First Aid: 🚑 Know how to respond to overdoses or adverse reactions—call emergency services if needed.
